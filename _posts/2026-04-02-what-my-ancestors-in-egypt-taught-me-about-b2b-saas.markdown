Yesterday at the seder table, my family and I read the ancient wisdom of the Haggadah and I kept noticing the same thing - this text had lots to say about improving B2B SaaS start-ups. Here's a summary of the insights I was able to share with them last night.

The four questions? More like the four Series:

Series A: **Why do we eat matzah when on other nights we eat bread?** Series A is about moving fast - for some, too fast. You'll have to leave parts of your business behind in order to create space for growth. Like how my ancestors ran for their lives and freedom, in a Series A startup you're running for something just as important - the two or three venture capital firms who have the largest ownership stakes in your company. And they don't care if you have some bread already cooking. Take that bread out, run, and eat it as matzah!

Series B: **Why on this night do we eat bitter herbs?** Your startup at this stage isn't always going to be fun. Sometimes you're going to have to buckle down and do the dirty work yourself. When a bitter task is critical, sometimes you won't be able to find some idiot on the job market to do it for you. In those cases, you have to step in and eat the bitter herbs yourself!

Series C: **On other nights, we don't even dip once, but tonight we dip twice. Why?** At the seder table, we dip vegetables in salt water as a reminder of the tears of my enslaved ancestors. In our daily standup meeting, we recount the trials and tribulations of the last day in much the same way.

Series D: **Why on this night do we recline while eating?** As a startup founder with a growth mindset, it can be difficult to find time to prioritize your own health and rest. If you want more help resting, consider buying my 35 dollar e-book on how to rest more effectively in your precious and limited downtime.

As I voiced these insights throughout the festive meal, I got lots of positive feedback from the table. Almost everything I said "clicked" right away - no follow up questions needed!

I hope this was as insightful to you as it was to ME!
